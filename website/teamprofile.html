Group Processes


The collaboration of our group in Assignment 2 is quite good. Although there were some problems at the beginning when our communication skill was the main problem, we could come through and finish Assignment 2 with a good result. In Assignment 3, we decided to make some changes to the Group Process. We believed each person in the group should have a specific role in the Group Assignment. It will help each of our concentrates in a particular area and thrive to achieve phenomenal outcomes. Besides, that is the way of how we will work in the future in the company after graduating. Therefore, I believe this change will help us have a good result of this assignment and have the right mindset in the workplace environment in the future. Secondly, we will divide the whole big project into small tasks, and we will have small deadlines for each of those. This change is like a compass, which can help the Lead Developer keep track of each member's. This can also allow us to do our tasks early to have a better result rather than start working so late, and the product is awful.



Career Plans


The ideal jobs of every member have not changed since the Assignment 1 and Assignment 2:

Minh: Full Stack Developer
Trung: Full Stack Developer
Anh: Test Analyst
Dat: Back end Developer

Common elements between jobs:

The most noticeable similarity between our ideal jobs is that 3 of us desire to work in the web development sector with 2 guys who wish to be full-stack developers and 1 guy wants to be a backend developer. The special case is Quang Anh as his ideal job is a test analyst. Fullstack developers (Minh and Trung) are considered to be jack-of-all-trades with the knowledge of both frontend (HTML, CSS) and backend (PHP), while backend developers (Dat) specialized in dealing with backend problems. In essence, our jobs share the same required skill sets.

Differences between the jobs

While full-stack and backend developers have many tools in common, Quang Anh’s job as a Test Analyst requires him to tackle many different problems, thus creating a much different toolset. The job is more like a delivery guy, who has to get the source from the front end side and test it in numerous scenarios, report the problems and then transfer them back to the back end to figure out a solution and repeat until everything works. You can say the full-stack developers (both frontend and backend) are the chefs and the test analysts are the delivery guy and the customer. Similar or different of each member career plans across the group

As mentioned in Assignment 2, our career paths are technically the same. We all wish to study hard at RMIT and then apply for numerous tech companies that are suitable for our field of interest. In conclusion, we all try to find a suitable job after university and work as an employee.
